Dincharya

Dinacharya is a daily routine designed to maintain and connect us to our circadian rhythms or the internal body clock.

  • Sooryodaya (5:00-8:30 am) - Yoga and meditation during this period helps the body to take in good amount of vitamin D. Plus a clean and healthy start will keep your energy levels up the entire day.
  • Breakfast (8:00-9:30 am) - Our breakfast should be unrefined, unprocessed,wholesome and natural. 
  • Mid-Morning Break (11:00-11:30 am) - This is the lightest meal of our entire day. its important that it detoxifies and nourishes our body
  • Lunch (1:00-2:30 pm) - This is the most important meal of the day and should be well balanced and nourishing. 
  • Evening Meal (4:00-4:30 pm) - This meal is more of a drink time; semi-solids and beverages are perfect for refreshing evenings 
  • Dinner (6:00-7:30 pm) - At dinner time light, gut-friendly food options in controlled portions should be preferred.
  • Bed Time (9:00-10:30 pm) - this is the best time to have any type of healthy hot beverage so it can give maximum benefit to the body.

INGREDIENTS- Oats, wheat flakes, cornflakes, fox nuts, almonds, walnuts, flax seeds, sesame seeds, pumpkin seeds, melon seeds, rock salt to taste.

    RECIPE-
  1. 1) Dry roast all the ingredients in a heated pan separately till they turn light brown except flakes.
  2. 2) Put them in a mixing bowl.
  3. 3) Add flakes and rock salt according to taste
  4. 4) Mix all the ingredients thoroughly until even distribution is done.
  5. 5) Store it in an airtight container

Benifits of Yoga

The relaxation techniques incorporated in yoga can lessen chronic pain, such as lower back pain, arthritis, headaches and carpal tunnel syndrome,” explains Dr. Nevins. “Yoga can also lower blood pressure and reduce insomnia.

    Other physical benefits of yoga include:
  • increased flexibility
  • increased muscle strength and tone
  • improved respiration, energy and vitality
  • maintaining a balanced metabolism
  • weight reduction
  • cardio and circulatory health
  • improved athletic performance
  • protection from injury

Aside from the physical benefits, one of the best benefits of yoga is how it helps a person manage stress, which is known to have devastating effects on the body and mind. “Stress can reveal itself in many ways, including back or neck pain, sleeping problems, headaches, drug abuse, and an inability to concentrate,” says Dr. Nevins. “Yoga can be very effective in developing coping skills and reaching a more positive outlook on life.”
